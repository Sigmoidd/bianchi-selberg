\begin{abstract}
We develop a machine-verified method for excluding Laplace eigenvalues
in $(0,1)$ on cusped hyperbolic $3$-orbifolds, combining a
Lax--Phillips cusp reduction, guaranteed Crouzeix--Raviart
finite-element lower bounds with fully explicit constants, and a
floating-point positive-definiteness certificate in the sense of Rump.
The method makes no use of the Selberg trace formula.  As a first
application we prove: the Picard orbifold
$\mathrm{PSL}(2,\ZZ[i])\backslash\HH^3$ has no Laplace eigenvalue in
$(0,1)$; equivalently $\lambda_1\ge 1$.  Every analytic constant is
enclosed in ball arithmetic and the final certificate is a
deterministic, reproducible computation.
\note{Abstract: stress (a) exclusion vs.\ existence — certified Maass
computations (BSV) prove existence; exclusion needs a different
architecture; (b) independence from the trace formula; (c) the
machinery is the point — Paper II applies it where the statement is
new.}
\end{abstract}

\item Reading guide + reproducibility pointer.

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:main}
The Laplace--Beltrami operator on
$L^2(\mathrm{PSL}(2,\ZZ[i])\backslash \HH^3)$ has no eigenvalue in
$(0,1)$.  Combined with the spectral decomposition, its spectrum in
$(0,1)$ is empty: $\lambda_1\ge 1$.
\end{theorem}

\item The criterion (positivity of $\mathcal{A}_s$ on
  $\{\mathcal{L}_s=0\}$) and the unconstrained pencil form $(P_s)$
  with the free parameter $\rho$ (Lemma P).
\item Remark: the relaxation philosophy — testing over all of $H^1(K)$
  drops the orbifold identifications; validity is one line, and it is
  what makes the discrete stage clean.
\end{itemize}

\section{The guaranteed lower bound}\label{sec:G1}
\note{Source: lower\_bound\_theory.md (Theorem G1 + lemmas), incl.\ the
$\tau_h=0$ remark and the exact-main-term refinement from
CONGRUENCE.md \S7 — PRESENT BOTH AS THE PRIMARY ROUTE here (cleaner

Reviewer job (ii): this section.}
\begin{itemize}
\item CR space and interpolation: (I2) projection property;
  \textbf{Lemma I1} (self-contained interpolation constant
  $\kappa=\sqrt{1/\pi^2+1/15}$ on \emph{arbitrary} tetrahedra; inputs:
  Payne--Weinberger 1960 / Bebendorf 2003 only).  Remark: sharper
  published $\sqrt{1/\pi^2+1/120}$ (CZZ20) optional, not load-bearing.
\item \textbf{Lemma D0} (exact trace reproduction): the cusp
  cross-section is tiled by mesh faces and $I_{CR}$ matches face means,
  so $t(v-I_{CR}v)=0$ — no trace error terms, uninflated boundary
  blocks.  [[Historically discovered late; expositionally it goes
  first.]]
\item Exact main terms / sandwiched error terms: $M_{ex}$, $Q_{rem}$,
  $\rho_w$, the $\theta_4$ split; error lemmas (E-M), (E-a); trace
  identity Lemma T (used for the sliver and as a tool).
\item Sliver absorption Lemma S (column means, per-face heights).
\item Window sweep: worst-case $(\lambda^+,\beta^+,\Delta\kappa)$ per
  $s$-window; monotonicity.
\item \textbf{Theorem G1} (master): the criterion follows from, per
  window, two scalar inequalities and one matrix inequality
  $N_h-D_h\succeq 0$ on the CR space.  Full proof.
\item Efficiency remark: all corrections are $O(h)$ or $O(h^2)$; the
  discrete pencil converges to the (relaxed) continuum problem.
\end{itemize}

\section{The verified certificate}\label{sec:cert}
\note{Sources: m3\_certify.py; lower\_bound\_theory.md \S5; Rump BIT 46
(2006) in refs/.  Reviewer job (iii): this section.  INCLUDE the two
refinements that Paper II stress-tested into existence: they are
machinery and belong to the Hammer.}
\begin{itemize}
\item Exactness conventions: mesh coordinates are IEEE doubles (exact
  rationals); geometry admissibility re-verified in ball arithmetic.
\item Interval enclosures: Taylor-moment enclosures for the weighted
  functionals ($\ell_0$, $M_{ex}$, $\int_T y^{-1}$) with rigorous
  remainder balls; consistency checks (row sums of $M_{ex}$ vs
  $\ell$).
\item \textbf{Lemma R} (factored interval rank-one reduction).
\item Rump's certificate, implemented literally: Thm 2.3 ($\Delta(A)$,
  $\gamma_{s(i,j)+2}$, underflow term), Cor 2.4, Lemma 2.5
  ($\varphi$-trick); ``any library routine can be used''.
\item \textbf{Refinement 1: error-free diagonal congruence.}  Scaling
  $A\mapsto SAS$ with $s_i=2^{\mathrm{round}(-\log_2 a_{ii}/2)}$ is
  exact in IEEE-754, preserves definiteness, equilibrates the diagonal;
  it removes the mismatch between Rump's stiffness-scale shift and the
  mass-scale eigenvalue floor.
\item \textbf{Refinement 2: per-row radius absorption.}  For symmetric
  $|E|\le R$ entrywise, $E\succeq-\mathrm{diag}(r)$, $r_i=\sum_j
  R_{ij}$ — replaces the uniform max-row-sum shift.  One-line proof
  ($2|x_ix_j|\le x_i^2+x_j^2$).
\item The frozen certificate for Theorem~\ref{thm:main}: parameter
  table (mesh, $Y$, $\nu^*$, Young parameters, $\rho$, precision),
  per-window results, runtimes; the parameter-search/rigorous-freeze
  discipline (floats choose, arb verifies).
\end{itemize}
